Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how innovation and responsibility have shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of technological innovation.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel introduces the 4004, the world’s first commercial microprocessor, sparking the modern computing revolution.

1978

8086 Processor

The 8086 processor launches the x86 architecture, which becomes the foundation of modern personal computing.

1985

386 Processor

Intel releases the 386 processor, introducing 32-bit computing and significantly improving multitasking performance.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel reaches its highest operational greenhouse gas emissions, later accelerating investments in clean energy and efficiency initiatives.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches the RISE strategy to drive responsible, inclusive, and sustainable progress across its global operations.

2022

Net-Zero by 2040

Intel commits to achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across global operations by 2040.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide, significantly reducing carbon emissions.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, bringing together global partners to collaborate on sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.
